Policy rates are the official central-bank rates published daily by the Bank for International Settlements (the US rate is the mid-point of the Fed's target range) and refresh every time this page is opened. "Last change" and "Stance" are computed automatically from that data: a bank is shown as cutting/hiking if its last interest-rate move was within the past eight months, otherwise as on hold (this refers to the policy interest rate only, not quantitative easing or tightening). Meeting dates are the officially published 2026 schedules of each central bank (decision/announcement day; collected July 2026) — click a bank's name to check its own calendar, which always takes precedence. CNY has no fixed meeting: China's loan prime rate is set monthly around the 20th. Dates highlighted in blue are within the next 14 days. This page is informational only — not investment advice.
